Monday

  • Client idea

    The first time we meet, both client and team speak about the project and visions in the short and long term.

  • Wireframe sketching

    Client and team sketch each page as they imagine how the project should be developed.

    The objetive of this stage is to extract everyone's main ideas (client and team) and through communication, come to an agreement of what needs to get done.

    When we speak of wireframe sketching, we literally mean a drawing of what you imagine your interface to be. A simple, pen and paper drawing. Check out an example!

    1. Everyone from our team and the client sketches, individually, the interfaces as imagined;

    2. The sketches should reflect the application's flow. How users should interact with the application, which elements must exist and things of that kind;

    3. Everyone presents their sketches and there's a discussion regarding the best wireframes;

    4. Criation of a final sketch, which becomes the delivery for the next fase.

  • Breaking stories into tasks and estimating

    The team disolves the final sketch in design and programming tasks, where each team member attributes his or her own estimate.

    The main objetive here is to find out what and how much effort is necessary for your development.

    1. After the team discusses the wifreframes, we create a step-by-step walkthrough for each wireframe. We call these "user stories" where inside each, there are a bunch of tasks;

    2. In the room where we works, we use drawing boards to list or draw all of the necessary components to create each wireframe (data models, etc);

    3. Each task has x points. Everyone on the team stipulates how much points they think the task is worth;

    4. The points used are always 1, 2, 4 or 8, which represent the team's effort in relation to a user story;

  • Prioritizing the tasks

    The client prioritizes the tasks that will get executed that week.

    After knowing the effort for each task, it's time to prioritize them and define which are the most important to get done for that week.

    1. The client re-arranges the tasks in order of most to least important, regardless of effort;

    2. The team helps the client prioritize, pointing out complexities of each of the tasks at hand;

    3. The client prioritizes an average of 30 points per person on the team. For example: a team with 2 people would deliver approximately 60 points;

    4. At the end of estimating, client and team know what will be developed during the week;

Tuesday and Wednesday

  • Development

    The team works on the tasks prioritized by the client.

    In development, the team starts to work on tasks that were defined every Mondays.

    1. The team are always communicating between themselves, this way everyone is aware of the project's progress;

    2. The project's code is owned and altered by all on the team. This way, everyone possesses working knowledge of the entire system;

    3. Don't worry! Your team will be having fun developing your project while eating snacks and drinking Red Bull;

  • Testing Environment

    The project becomes accessible for testing by the client at a pre-production environment.

    All projects have a testing environment, called staging. Staging works online so that client and the team executes their tests on an environment that's as most up-to-date as possible to what's on production.

    1. The staging environment is secured with a login credential so that only those with access rights may enter;

  • Best practices

    The team utilizes automated-testing and all other XP (Xtreme Programming - Agile Methodology) practices.

    The team utilizes a range of best practices: automated-testing, 100% coverage and continuous integration.

    1. Each line of code has been executed at least once in order to guarantee its functionality. In this way, new functionalities do not affect old ones;

    2. We have to test everything! After this, we 100% guarantee we'll cover your tests. In other words, there won't be one single line of code that has not been tested;

    3. Every small portion of code that is developed is sent to the staging environment, a process that happens multiple times per day, guaranteeing there is no surprises on your production environment.

Thursday

  • Completion of the development work

    On Thursday, all tasks previously prioritized on Mondays are concluded and are made available on the staging environment.

  • Testing Scripts

    At the end of the day, it's delivered a testing script to the client.

    With the completion of the development work on Thursday, the client is notified and receives a testing script to test the entire system.

    1. The testing script is a step-by-step guide on how to test what's been deployed on stating environment;

    2. The client annotates any observation regarding the delivery right on the testing script document;

  • Testing the system

    The client has from Thursday night to the following Monday to test what has been developed and bring back observations for the next meeting.
