Many of our solutions require the use of cookies, and because of this reason, they are used on our pages, emphasizing once more that this is due to technical criteria only, cookies are not used to collect or spy on others who seek information or use for such third-party cookies.

A cookie, also known as an http cookie, web cookie, or browser cookie, is a piece of data stored by a website within a browser, and then subsequently sent back to the same website by the browser. Cookies were designed to be a reliable mechanism for websites to remember things that a browser had done there in the past, which can include having clicked particular buttons, logging in, or having read pages on that site months or years ago.

Browsers can be configured to notify you before you receive a cookie, giving you the opportunity to decide whether or not to accept it, however, if not accepted, some sites may not function properly.

All stored information is controlled and restricted, never sold or transferred to others for any reason, even through surveys.
